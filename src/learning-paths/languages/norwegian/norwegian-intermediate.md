## Ordering at a Café

When ordering in Norwegian, politeness goes a long way. The key phrase is **"Jeg vil gjerne ha..."** (I would like...).

| Norwegian | English |
|-----------|---------|
| Jeg vil gjerne ha en kaffe | I would like a coffee |
| Med melk, vær så snill | With milk, please |
| Hva koster det? | How much does it cost? |
| Regningen, takk | The bill, please |
| Kan jeg få menyen? | Can I have the menu? |

Useful vocabulary:

| Norwegian | English |
|-----------|---------|
| Kaffe | Coffee |
| Te | Tea |
| Vann | Water |
| Smørbrød | Sandwich |
| Kake | Cake |

## Asking for Directions

Getting around requires understanding directions and common location words.

| Norwegian | English |
|-----------|---------|
| Hvor er...? | Where is...? |
| Til venstre | To the left |
| Til høyre | To the right |
| Rett fram | Straight ahead |
| Her | Here |
| Der | There |

Common places:

| Norwegian | English |
|-----------|---------|
| Jernbanestasjonen | The train station |
| Sykehuset | The hospital |
| Apoteket | The pharmacy |
| Butikken | The store |
| Restauranten | The restaurant |

Example conversation:

> **Unnskyld, hvor er jernbanestasjonen?**
>
> Excuse me, where is the train station?

> **Gå rett fram, og ta til venstre ved butikken.**
>
> Go straight ahead, and turn left by the store.

## Talking About Your Day

To describe routines and daily activities, Norwegian uses the **presens** (present tense), which is very regular.

| Norwegian | English |
|-----------|---------|
| Jeg står opp klokken syv | I wake up at seven |
| Jeg spiser frokost | I eat breakfast |
| Jeg går på jobb | I go to work |
| Jeg lager middag | I make dinner |
| Jeg legger meg | I go to bed |

To form the present tense, add **-r** to the verb stem:

| Verb | Stem | Present |
|------|------|---------|
| å snakke (to speak) | snakk- | snakker |
| å spise (to eat) | spis- | spiser |
| å bo (to live) | bo- | bor |
| å jobbe (to work) | jobb- | jobber |
