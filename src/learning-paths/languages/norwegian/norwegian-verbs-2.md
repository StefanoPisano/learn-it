## Perfektum (Present Perfect)

The present perfect connects a past action to the present. It is formed with **har** (have) + **past participle**.

### Formation

| Subject | har | Past participle |
|---------|-----|-----------------|
| Jeg | har | snakket |
| Du | har | snakket |
| Han/Hun | har | snakket |
| Vi | har | snakket |

### Past participle of regular verbs

| Group | Infinitive | Past participle |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|
| -e verbs | å snakke | snakket |
| -e verbs | å leve | levd |
| -re verbs | å bo | bodd |
| -e verbs | å elske | elsket |

> **规则:** Regular past participles are formed by adding **-t** to the past tense stem.

### Irregular past participles

| Infinitive | Past participle | Meaning |
|-----------|-----------------|---------|
| å være | vært | to be |
| å ha | hatt | to have |
| å kunne | kunnet | to be able to |
| å måtte | måttet | to have to |
| å gå | gått | to go |
| å se | sett | to see |
| å spise | spist | to eat |
| å drikke | drukket | to drink |

## When to Use Perfektum vs. Preteritum

This is one of the trickiest aspects of Norwegian grammar.

### Use perfektum when:

| Situation | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| The action has present relevance | Jeg **har mistet** nøkkelen (I have lost the key — I still don't have it) |
| The time is not specified | Vi **har vært** i Norge (We have been to Norway) |
| With "aldri" (never) | Jeg **har aldri** vært der (I have never been there) |
| With "allerede" (already) | Han **har allerede** spist (He has already eaten) |

### Use preteritum when:

| Situation | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Specific past time | Jeg **snakket** med ham **i går** (I spoke with him yesterday) |
| Sequence of events | Jeg **stod** opp, **spiste** og **gikk** på jobb |
| Narrating a story | Det **var** en gang... (Once upon a time...) |

> **Rule of thumb:** If you mention *when* it happened → preteritum. If not → perfektum.

## Pluskvamperfektum (Past Perfect)

The past perfect describes an action that happened **before** another past action. It is formed with **hadde** (had) + **past participle**.

### When to use it

| Situation | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Before another past action | Jeg **hadde allerede** spist da han kom (I had already eaten when he arrived) |
| Reported speech in past | Hun sa at hun **hadde vært** der før (She said she had been there before) |
| Conditional (if-clause) | Hvis jeg **hadde visst** det, hadde jeg kommet (If I had known, I would have come) |

### Formation

| Subject | hadde | Past participle |
|---------|-------|-----------------|
| Jeg | hadde | snakket |
| Du | hadde | snakket |
| Han/Hun | hadde | snakket |

> **Notice:** The past participle form is the same as in perfektum — only the auxiliary verb changes from "har" to "hadde".

## Betingelsesform (Conditional)

The conditional expresses hypothetical situations or polite requests. It is formed with **ville** (would) + **infinitive**.

### Common uses

| Use | Norwegian | English |
|-----|-----------|---------|
| Hypothetical | Jeg **ville** gjøre det | I would do it |
| Polite request | Kunne du **hjelpe** meg? | Could you help me? |
| Past condition | Hvis jeg **hadde** tid, ville jeg komme | If I had time, I would come |

### Conditional + past participle (would have)

| Norwegian | English |
|-----------|---------|
| Jeg **ville ha gjort** det | I would have done it |
| Vi **ville ha vunnet** | We would have won |

> **Formation:** ville + ha + past participle

## Imperativ (Imperative)

The imperative is used for commands and requests. It uses the **verb stem** (no subject, no ending).

### Formation

| Infinitive | Stem | Imperative |
|-----------|------|------------|
| å snakke | snakk- | Snakk! |
| å spise | spis- | Spis! |
| å gå | gå- | Gå! |
| å komme | kom- | Kom! |
| å se | se- | Se! |

### Polite requests

| Norwegian | English |
|-----------|---------|
| Vær så snill | Please (be so kind) |
| Kan du...? | Can you...? |
| Vil du...? | Will you...? |

> **Note:** Use "vær så snill" (please) to soften commands.

## Exercise: Translate to Norwegian

Translate these sentences using the correct tense:

1. I have spoken with him. (perfektum)
2. She had already eaten when I arrived. (pluskvamperfektum)
3. If I were you, I would go. (betingelsesform)
4. Come here! (imperativ)
5. We went to the store yesterday. (preteritum)

**Answers:**
1. Jeg har snakket med ham.
2. Hun hadde allerede spist da jeg kom.
3. Hvis jeg var deg, ville jeg gå.
4. Kom hit!
5. Vi gikk til butikken i går.
