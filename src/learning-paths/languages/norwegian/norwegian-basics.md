## Greetings and Common Phrases

Norwegian uses two written standards: **Bokmål** and **Nynorsk**. This course follows Bokmål, which is the most widely used.

Here are essential greetings:

| Norwegian | English |
|-----------|---------|
| Hei | Hello |
| God morgen | Good morning |
| God dag | Good day |
| God kveld | Good evening |
| Ha det | Goodbye |
| Takk | Thank you |
| Vær så snill | Please |
| Unnskyld | Excuse me / Sorry |

## Introducing Yourself

In Norwegian, you introduce yourself with **"Jeg heter..."** (My name is...).

| Norwegian | English |
|-----------|---------|
| Jeg heter... | My name is... |
| Jeg er fra... | I am from... |
| Jeg bor i... | I live in... |
| Hyggelig å møte deg | Nice to meet you |
| Snakker du engelsk? | Do you speak English? |

Example:

> **Hei! Jeg heter Anna. Jeg er fra Italia. Hyggelig å møte deg!**
>
> Hello! My name is Anna. I am from Italy. Nice to meet you!

## Numbers 1–10

Learning numbers is essential for everyday conversations, shopping, and telling time.

| Number | Norwegian |
|--------|-----------|
| 1 | en |
| 2 | to |
| 3 | tre |
| 4 | fire |
| 5 | fem |
| 6 | seks |
| 7 | syv |
| 8 | åtte |
| 9 | ni |
| 10 | ti |

> **Tip:** Norwegian numbers follow a regular pattern and are quite similar to English for small numbers.
