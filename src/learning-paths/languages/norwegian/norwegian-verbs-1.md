## Overview of Norwegian Verb Tenses

Norwegian has six main verb tenses. This learning path covers the first three:

| Tense | Norwegian | Use |
|-------|-----------|-----|
| Present | Presens | Now, habits, general truths |
| Future | Futurum | Future actions |
| Past | Preteritum | Completed past actions |

All regular verbs follow predictable conjugation patterns. Norwegian has **four verb groups** based on infinitive endings:

| Group | Infinitive ending | Examples |
|-------|-------------------|----------|
| -e verbs | -e | å snakke, å leve, å elske |
| -re verbs | -re | å sy, å bo |
| -je verbs | -je | å tro |
| Irregular | various | å være, å ha, å gå |

## Presens (Present Tense)

The present tense describes actions happening now, habits, or general truths.

### Regular conjugation

Add **-r** to the infinitive (drop the å):

| Infinitive | Present |
|-----------|---------|
| å snakke | jeg snakker |
| å leve | jeg lever |
| å jobbe | jeg jobber |
| å spise | jeg spiser |

> **All regular verbs** have the same form for all subjects:
> - Jeg snakker, du snakker, han snakker, vi snakker...

### Irregular present forms

| Infinitive | Present | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| å være | er | to be |
| å ha | har | to have |
| å kunne | kan | to be able to |
| å måtte | må | to have to |
| å ville | vil | to want |
| å skjule | vet | to know |

## Futurum (Future Tense)

Norwegian does not have a dedicated future verb form. Instead, you use several strategies:

### 1. Skal + infinitive (intention/plan)

| Norwegian | English |
|-----------|---------|
| Jeg **skal** studere i morgen | I will study tomorrow |
| Vi **skal** reise til Norge | We will travel to Norway |

> Use **skal** when you have decided or planned to do something.

### 2. Vil + infinitive (prediction/want)

| Norwegian | English |
|-----------|---------|
| Det **vil** regne i morgen | It will rain tomorrow |
| Jeg **vil** bli lege | I want to become a doctor |

> Use **vil** for predictions or expressing desires.

### 3. Kommer til å + infinitive (factual future)

| Norwegian | English |
|-----------|---------|
| Jeg **kommer til å** flytte | I am going to move |
| De **kommer til å** gifte seg | They are going to get married |

> Use **kommer til å** for factual statements about the future.

## Preteritum (Simple Past)

The preteritum describes completed actions in the past.

### Regular conjugation

The conjugation depends on the verb group:

**-e verbs:** Add **-de** to the stem

| Infinitive | Stem | Past |
|-----------|------|------|
| å snakke | snakk- | jeg snakket |
| å leve | lev- | jeg levde |
| å jobbe | jobb- | jeg jobbet |

**-re verbs:** Replace -e with **-a**

| Infinitive | Past |
|-----------|------|
| å sy | jeg sydde → jeg sydde |
| å bo | jeg bodde |

> **Note:** Many -re verbs are irregular.

### Irregular past forms (very common!)

| Infinitive | Past | Meaning |
|-----------|------|---------|
| å være | var | to be |
| å ha | hadde | to have |
| å kunne | kunne | to be able to |
| å måtte | måtte | to have to |
| å gå | gikk | to go |
| å se | så | to see |
| å spise | spiste | to eat |
| å drikke | drakk | to drink |

## Exercise: Conjugate the Verbs

Fill in the present and past tense for these verbs:

| Infinitive | Present | Past |
