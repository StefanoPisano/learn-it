## Compound Words (Sammensetninger)

Norwegian frequently combines two or more words into **compound words**. This is one of the most distinctive features of Scandinavian languages.

The rule is simple: join the words together.

| Components | Compound | Meaning |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| bil (car) + vei (road) | bilvei | car road |
| hånd (hand) + sko (shoe) | handsko | glove |
| hus (house) + nøkkel (key) | husnøkkel | house key |
| bok (book) + butikk (store) | bokbutikk | bookstore |

> **Important:** The last word determines the gender and meaning of the compound.
>
> - *handsko* is masculine because *sko* is masculine
> - *bokbutikk* is feminine because *butikk* is feminine

## Definite Articles (Bestemte Artikler)

Norwegian definite articles are **suffixes** attached to the noun, not separate words (unlike English "the").

| Gender | Indefinite | Definite | Pattern |
|--------|-----------|----------|---------|
| Masculine | en bil (a car) | bil**en** (the car) | -en |
| Feminine | en bok (a book) | boka / bok**en** (the book) | -a / -en |
| Neuter | et hus (a house) | hus**et** (the house) | -et |

For **plural** forms:

| Gender | Indefinite Plural | Definite Plural |
|--------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Masculine | biler (cars) | bil**ene** (the cars) |
| Feminine | bøker (books) | bok**ene** (the books) |
| Neuter | hus (houses) | hus**ene** (the houses) |

> **Note:** Some nouns change their stem vowel in plural (vokalskift):
> - *mann* → *menn* (man → men)
> - *fot* → *føtter* (foot → feet)

## Subordinate Clauses (Leddsetninger)

Advanced Norwegian requires understanding **subordinate clauses** introduced by conjunctions.

Common subordinating conjunctions:

| Norwegian | English | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| fordi | because | Jeg er trøtt **fordi** jeg jobbet lenge |
| hvis | if | **Hvis** det regner, blir jeg hjemme |
| når | when | **Når** jeg kommer hjem, lager jeg middag |
| selv om | even though | **Selv om** det er kaldt, går jeg tur |
| at | that | Jeg tror **at** han kommer |

> **Word order rule:** In subordinate clauses, the **verb moves to the end**.
>
> - Main clause: *Jeg jobber fordi jeg **må*** (I work because I must)
> - Notice how "må" moves to the end.

## Passive Voice (Passiv)

Norwegian has two ways to form the passive voice:

### 1. With "bli" (to become)

> Bilen **ble vasket** av mannen.
> The car was washed by the man.

### 2. With "-s" suffix

> Bilen **vaskes** hver mandag.
> The car is washed every Monday.

| Active | Passive (bli) | Passive (-s) |
|--------|---------------|--------------|
| Jeg lager mat | Maten ble laget | Maten lages |
| De bygger huset | Huset ble bygget | Huset bygges |
| Han skriver boka | Boka ble skrevet | Boka skrives |

> **When to use which:**
> - **-s form:** General statements, routines, formal writing
> - **ble + past participle:** Specific events, narrative past
